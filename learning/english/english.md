* Adjectives and prepositions

  : with at we use at with adjectives like good/bad/amazing/brilliant/terriable to talk aboyt skill and abilites

  ex :
  - He's reall good a english
  - She's anazing at the piano
  - They're terriable at organining anything

  : with about often use about with adjectives of feelings like angry/exeited/happy/nervous/stressed/worried to explain what is causing that feeling

  ex :
  - I'm angry about the decision
  - He's nervoues about the persentation

  : with of however, sometimes we use of with feelings,

  ex :
  - She was afraid of telling her mum
  - I'm fightened of having an accident

  : with to we can use to to show the connection between people or things

  ex :
  - He's married ot the dirctor

  ex :
  - Stress is bad for you
  - The town is famous for its cheese

  : with in

  ex :
  - She's interested in the porject
  - They want someone who's experienced in design

* Adjectives ending in 'ed' and 'ing'

  : ed adjectives that end in 'ed' generally describe emotions - they tell us how people feel

  ex :
  - I was so bored in that lesson, I almost fell asleep
  - He was surprised to see Helen ofter all those years

  : ing adjectives that end in 'ing' generally describe the thing that causes the emotion - a boring lesson makes you feel bored

  ex :
  - Have you seen that film? It's really frightening
  - I could listen to her for hours. She's so intersting

* Articles : the or no article

  : no article we don't normally use an article in expressions with school, university, prison hospital, bed, work, home

  ex :
  - go to bed be in bed go to work start work go home start school go to university

  : the we usually use the if someone it just visiting the place, and not there as a student/prisoner/patient

  ex :
  - My son has started school now. I went to the school to meet his teacher
  - I went to the prison a lot when i was a social worker
